# ICT Strategy — Trading Philosophy of this Bot

This document encodes *what* the bot trades and *why*. The mathematical formalization of each concept lives in [`docs/concepts/`](./docs/concepts/) (one file per concept, populated during Phase 1).

---

## 1. Worldview

The market is modeled as a centralized **Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm (IPDA)** that moves price toward liquidity and price inefficiencies on a strict time schedule. Therefore:

- **Time is the primary catalyst**, price the coordinate. The bot scans for setups *only* inside defined killzones and macros; outside them, the expected value of any signal degrades and the engine sits flat.
- The market cycles through **Accumulation → Manipulation (Judas) → Distribution** (the *Power of Three*). The bot biases entries against the manipulation leg.
- Two regimes split every dealing range: **Premium** (sells) and **Discount** (buys). The bot does not buy in premium nor sell in discount.

## 2. Layered analytical model

| Layer | Purpose | Timeframes | Modules |
| ----- | ------- | ---------- | ------- |
| **Bias** | Daily/weekly direction, liquidity targets, major PD Arrays | D, H4, H1 | `structure/`, `signals/liquidity/`, `signals/ranges/` |
| **Trigger** | MSS after a liquidity sweep into a high-timeframe PD Array | M15, M5 | `structure/`, `signals/imbalance/`, `signals/blocks/` |
| **Entry** | Limit order at FVG / OB / Breaker, confirmed by L2 microstructure | M3, M1, L2 ticks | `signals/setups/`, L2 confirmation layer |
| **Risk** | SL behind the structural extreme, size from SL distance | — | `risk/` |
| **Exit** | Opposite liquidity pool or higher-timeframe PD Array | matches Bias | `signals/liquidity/`, `signals/ranges/` |

The bot must establish Bias *before* it is allowed to act on a Trigger, and a Trigger *before* an Entry — this is the **top-down validation protocol**.

## 3. PD Array hierarchy (priority)

Higher-priority PD Arrays override lower ones at the same price.

1. Prior structural high/low
2. Breaker Block
3. Mitigation Block
4. Opening Gap (daily/weekly)
5. FVG (BISI/SIBI)
6. Order Block (with Mean Threshold)
7. Rejection Block

A PD Array detected in isolation on a low timeframe **without** a higher-timeframe target context is rejected by the engine to reduce false positives.

## 4. Validation rules (universal)

- **Bodies, not wicks, define invalidation.** A wick beyond a level is a liquidity grab; a body close beyond it invalidates the level.
- **Displacement requires an imbalance.** A move that breaks structure but leaves no FVG is downgraded to noise.
- **Midnight Open filter.** Longs only below the day's 00:00 NY open; shorts only above. Prevents buying expensive and selling cheap.
- **Killzones gate the engine.** No new orders outside London KZ, NY AM KZ, and NY PM KZ. Hard flatten at 16:30 NY.
- **High-impact news (e.g., 08:30 NY)** disables active entries and may invalidate PD Arrays formed before the print.

## 5. Setups (V1 catalog)

Each setup is a *composition* of detectors with explicit confluence rules. Listed in order of expected priority for V1:

1. **Unicorn Model** — Breaker Block ∩ FVG, ideally inside OTE (Fib 0.62–0.79, sweet spot 0.705).
2. **MSS + FVG entry** — Liquidity sweep, MSS with displacement, retrace into the FVG generated by the displacement leg.
3. **OB + OTE** — Bullish/bearish OB located inside the OTE zone of the higher-timeframe dealing range.
4. **Silver Bullet (NY AM 10:00–11:00)** — Time-anchored setup inside the NY killzone, post-sweep entry on FVG.
5. **PO3 Daily Model** — Reverse Judas swing at session open, entry on retrace through a session PD Array.

Setups not in this list are not traded by V1 of the bot — even if they look attractive — until their formalization passes review.

## 6. Risk doctrine

- Position size is derived from **SL distance × dollar-per-tick × per-trade risk %**. No fixed lot sizes.
- If the required SL exceeds the per-trade risk cap, the setup is skipped — never trimmed.
- Daily loss limit hard-stops new entries for the day.
- Max consecutive losses pause the engine until manual reactivation.

## 7. What this bot will not do

- Trade outside killzones, even on a clean-looking setup.
- Take an OB or FVG without a higher-timeframe context driving it.
- Override the Midnight Open filter.
- Average down. ICT entries are single-shot at a defined price with a fixed SL.
- Go to live execution before walk-forward + Monte Carlo bootstrap reports are reviewed.
